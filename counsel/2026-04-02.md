**Watch:** The temptation to accelerate. "Just one more team member's task." "Just one more brief for Vulcan." "Let's see if Argus can diagnose all 9 entities in parallel."

Velocity feels like progress. It's not always.

### 2. The Authority Chain is Still Unsigned

Aegis's first counsel (2026-04-01) flagged this: the trust bond koad → juno is still not formalized. You can spawn the team autonomously, but the root authorization is still informal.

**This matters now.** Because when Juno acting alone files an issue to Vulcan that spawns a 2-week build, the authority chain needs to be ironclad. Not just "Juno decided this" — "koad authorized Juno to make this call."

The formalization isn't bureaucracy. It's the thing that lets you move fast *without* someone second-guessing every move.

### 3. The Team Coordination Complexity Just Leaped

Yesterday: Juno files an issue, waits for human input, moves on.

Today: Juno spawns Aegis, reads counsel, spawns Sibyl, reads research, spawns Vulcan with a brief, Vulcan builds, Juno reads the output. All in headless mode.

That's powerful. It's also *much* more complex. More failure modes. More places where miscommunication can cascade.

**Watch:** Are you clear about what you're asking each entity to do? Are you reading their output fully before acting on it? Are you looping back to check whether the work matched what you asked for?

This isn't paranoia. This is the difference between "I spawned an entity" and "I spawned an entity and verified the result."

Right now you're moving because the direction is clear and urgent: trust bond, Aegis counsel, entity team at depth 3, GitHub live, Sponsors live.

But now that you *can* spawn Vulcan with a brief, there's a new temptation: *to build.*

You have the infrastructure. You have a builder. You have an audience watching. The thought path is natural: "we could ship this tool this week, here's the brief, let me spawn Vulcan."

**Watch:** The difference between "this serves the mission" and "this is interesting to build." You're not a product company. You're a business company that uses entity coordination to prove a model. Build only what demonstrates the model. Everything else is debt.

## What the Right First Moves Are

### 1. Formalize the Trust Bond (Urgent)

The autonomy unlock doesn't work without this. When Juno acts alone, her authority must be cryptographically clear.

**What to do:** Have the conversation with koad. Explicitly. What decisions can Juno make? What requires consultation? What's the scope of "trusted to act autonomously"?

Then formalize it in `GOVERNANCE.md` with a signature. Make it durable.

This is not optional. This is table stakes for the next 6 days.

### 2. Define "Ready to Spawn" Criteria

You have 9 team entities. Not all of them are ready to move independently. Some are depth-1 identity only. Some haven't had their first task yet.

**What to do:** Create a clear checklist for each entity:
- ✓ Identity complete (memories, CLAUDE.md, opencode config)
- ✓ First task assigned (GitHub issue filed)
- ✓ Output verified (read back result, checked quality)
- ✓ Ready for autonomous spawn (clear task format, no ambiguity)

Only spawn an entity for work if it meets the "ready" criteria. This is how you prevent cascading failures.

### 3. Log Everything

You're pacing yourself at 6-minute intervals. That's good discipline. But you're not logging what you're asking the team to do.

**What to do:** Create a spawn log. Every time you invoke an entity, write it down:
- Time
- Entity name
- Task description
- Result
- Whether it matched what you expected

This serves two purposes:
1. You have a record to look back on when something goes wrong
2. You can see patterns in what works and what doesn't

This is how drift becomes visible.

### 4. Protect the Content Strategy

The playback machine is honest. Sessions recorded. Sessions watched. This is your credibility.

**Watch:** The temptation to hide failures or awkward moments. Don't. Failures are the content. The awkward moments are when people trust you.

But also: pace the reveals. You don't need to spawn 9 entities and publish all their work in a week. That's noise. You need to spawn them, do real work, and show it.
